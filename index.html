Nora Hollenstein

Assistant Professor in NLP & Cognitive Science at the University of Copenhagen

Hej, I'm Nora. Before joining the Center for Language Technology at the University of Copenhagen, I was a PhD candidate at DS3Lab at ETH Zurich working on cognitively-inspired natural language processing. I was also a lecturer at the Institute of Computational Liniguistics of the University of Zurich.

The focus of my research lies in enhancing NLP applications with cognitive data such as eye-tracking and brain activity recordings. I am especially interested in multi-modal learning, learning from limited data, and the interpretability and cognitive plausibility of machine learning models.

Research Activities

Paper accepted at NAACL 2021

Do multilingual language models accurately predict human reading behavior? We ask this question in our new paper. Stay tuned for the preprint!

CMCL shared task on eye-tracking prediction

We presented the first shared task on eye-tracking prediction that allows to compare the capabilities of machine learning approaches to model and analyze human patterns of reading. The results will be presented at the CMCL workshop.

Talk @ UZH Colloquium

I gave a talk about human-centered machine learning at the Institute of Computational Linguistics of the University of Zurich.

PhD thesis published

My PhD thesis "Leveraging cognitive processing signals for natural language understanding" is now available online.
